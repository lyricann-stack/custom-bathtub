Acrylic, stone resin or copper? An honest guide.

Updated June 2026 · ~5 min read · Kreiner Atelier

Three materials dominate the freestanding bathtub world: acrylic, stone resin (cast stone), and copper. Each has a place — but for most real bathrooms, premium acrylic quietly wins on the things you live with every day.

Weight & installation

This is where the gap is widest. A freestanding acrylic tub weighs 27–45 kg, so two people can carry it upstairs and it sits on standard floors without reinforcement. Stone resin runs 90–160 kg and copper can reach 270 kg — both often need reinforced floors, professional handling and curbside-only freight.

Warmth & comfort

Acrylic has very low thermal conductivity, so the surface feels warm the moment you touch it and insulates the water well. Copper and stone feel cold to first touch. Stone resin's one genuine edge is thermal mass on a very long soak — for everyday baths the difference is small.

Design freedom

Acrylic is thermoformed over a mould, making it the most shapeable tub material there is — almost any curve is achievable, which is exactly why true customisation is possible. Stone resin is limited to a fixed range of cast shapes; copper to a narrow set of hand-formed classics.

Price & value

Acrylic is the most affordable premium tub material; stone resin sits at a premium and copper is the most expensive — commonly AUD $3,000 to $15,000+ before installation. You spend on the design, not the dead weight.

The honest verdict

Choose copper for a statement heirloom with no budget ceiling. Choose stone resin for maximum thermal mass and a dense, monolithic feel — if your floor can take it. For everyone else, and especially for a custom shape, an upper-floor bathroom and a sensible budget, premium acrylic is the smart choice. It's why we build exclusively in acrylic.
