Acrylic, solid surface or copper? An honest guide.

Updated July 2026 · ~5 min read · Kreiner Atelier

Three materials dominate the freestanding bathtub world: acrylic, solid surface (cast mineral composite), and copper. Each has a place. We craft bespoke tubs in the first two, so this guide is about matching the material to your bathroom — not selling one over the other.

Weight & installation

This is where the practical gap is widest. A freestanding acrylic tub weighs 27–45 kg, so two people can carry it upstairs and it sits on standard floors without reinforcement. Solid surface runs 90–150 kg and copper can reach 270 kg — plan for ground-floor placement or a floor-loading check, and professional handling on delivery. We advise on this as part of every solid surface project.

Warmth & comfort

Acrylic has very low thermal conductivity, so the surface feels warm the moment you touch it and insulates the water well — ideal for everyday baths. Solid surface starts cooler to first touch but has real thermal mass: once warmed it holds heat through a long, spa-style soak better than anything else we make. Copper holds heat too, but is cold to first touch.

Design freedom

Acrylic is thermoformed over a mould — almost any flowing curve is achievable. Solid surface is CNC-shaped and hand-finished, which excels at crisp, sculptural profiles and seamless integrated details. Because we tool both in-house, true customisation from your sketch or CAD file is possible in either; copper remains limited to a narrow set of hand-formed classics.

Price & value

Acrylic is the most affordable premium tub material. Solid surface sits at a premium for its mass, machining and hand-finishing. Copper is the most expensive — commonly AUD $3,000 to $15,000+ before installation. Because we are factory-direct on both acrylic and solid surface, you spend on the design and the craft, not on middlemen.

The honest verdict

Choose copper for a statement heirloom with no budget ceiling. Choose solid surface for maximum thermal mass, a dense monolithic feel and invisible repairs — if your floor can take it. Choose acrylic for an upper-floor bathroom, flowing organic curves, a warmer first touch and a friendlier budget. We craft the last two ourselves, so tell us about your bathroom and we'll recommend honestly — the custom shape is yours either way.
