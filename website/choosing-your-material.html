Acrylic or solid surface: which is right for you?

Updated July 2026 · ~5 min read · Kreiner Atelier

We craft bespoke bathtubs in both premium acrylic and solid surface — so this guide has no side to sell. The custom shape is the same either way; the material decides how your tub feels, weighs and lives. Here is how we help clients choose.

In this guide
  1. Look & feel
  2. Weight & installation
  3. Warmth & the soak
  4. Shape freedom
  5. Care, repairs & longevity
  6. Price
  7. How to decide

1 · Look & feel

Acrylic reads warm and luminous — a smooth, slightly glossy skin available in any colour, from gloss white to matte black to a colour-matched hue. Solid surface reads dense and mineral: a soft matte, stone-like surface that feels carved rather than formed, with a seamless, monolithic presence. Neither is "better" — they are two different design languages.

2 · Weight & installation

A freestanding acrylic tub weighs 27–45 kg: two people can carry it upstairs and it sits on standard floors without reinforcement. Solid surface runs 90–150 kg — a grounded, substantial piece that usually calls for ground-floor placement or a floor-loading check, and professional handling on delivery. If your bathroom is upstairs in a timber-framed home, acrylic is often the practical answer; on a slab, either works.

3 · Warmth & the soak

Acrylic has very low thermal conductivity — warm the moment you touch it, and a good insulator of bathwater. Solid surface starts cooler to the touch but has real thermal mass: once warmed, it holds heat beautifully through a long spa-style soak. Quick daily baths favour acrylic; slow evening rituals favour solid surface.

4 · Shape freedom

Both take genuine customisation — that is the point of the atelier. Acrylic is thermoformed over a mould, which makes flowing, organic curves almost limitless. Solid surface is CNC-shaped and hand-finished, which excels at crisp edges, sculptural profiles and seamless integrated details. We build both directly from your sketch, photo or CAD file.

5 · Care, repairs & longevity

Both wear well for decades with simple care. Acrylic scratches can be polished or filled and blended. Solid surface has a quiet superpower: because the colour runs all the way through, it can be sanded and re-polished back to factory-new — repairs are effectively invisible.

6 · Price

For a comparable size and design, acrylic is the more affordable of the two — lighter material and lighter freight. Solid surface sits at a premium for its mass, machining and hand-finishing. Both are factory-direct with no middlemen, and financing is available on either.

7 · How to decide

Choose acrylic for an upper-floor bathroom, flowing organic curves, a warmer first touch and a friendlier budget. Choose solid surface for a ground-floor statement piece, a dense mineral feel, long spa soaks and invisible repairs. Still unsure? Send us your floor plan and the mood you're after — we'll recommend the material honestly, because we make both.
