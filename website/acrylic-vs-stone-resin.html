Acrylic vs stone resin: which is right for you?

Updated June 2026 · ~6 min read · Kreiner Atelier

Premium brands have spent years framing acrylic as the "cheap" option. Here is the honest, point-by-point comparison so you can decide for your own bathroom — not theirs.

In this guide
  1. Shape customisation
  2. Weight & installation
  3. Price & financing
  4. Warmth, comfort & repairs
  5. Returns & delivery
  6. The verdict

1 · Shape customisation

This is the biggest practical difference, and it rarely makes the brochure. Most so-called custom bathtub builders online only let you change size and colour; the silhouette is fixed. Acrylic is heat-formed over a mould, so a genuinely new curve is a question of tooling, not chemistry — which is why we manufacture directly from a customer's or designer's CAD file. If a brand only offers size and colour, it isn't customisation — it's a relabelled catalogue.

2 · Weight & installation

Stone resin is heavy — commonly 200–350 lb empty. Many brands deliver curbside only, leaving you to arrange a 3–4-person crew and possibly reinforce the floor. Acrylic is the lightest tub material by a wide margin: a comfortable two-person lift that fits standard residential floors and is far cheaper to freight.

3 · Price & financing

For a comparable size and finish, custom acrylic typically runs 30–50% less than stone resin — partly the material, partly lighter freight, partly cutting out middlemen with factory-direct production. A $4,000 custom tub can sit around ~AUD $130/month with Afterpay or Zip.

4 · Warmth, comfort & repairs

Acrylic feels warm to the touch and holds heat well. On durability both wear well — the difference is repairs: a scratch or chip in acrylic can usually be polished or filled and blended, while damage in stone resin is harder to fix invisibly.

5 · Returns & delivery

Heavy stone-resin tubs often carry punishing return terms — a short window, customer-paid freight both ways, and a restocking fee that can total $600–$900 on a $3,000 tub. Because every Kreiner tub is bespoke, we sign off a spec before production, so there are no surprises to return — and we are made and serviced in Australia.

6 · The verdict

Choose stone resin if you specifically want the dense, monolithic feel of cast stone and you are happy inside a fixed catalogue. Choose custom acrylic if you want a genuinely original shape, a lighter install, easier repairs, a warmer soak and a lower price with financing — and, in Australia or New Zealand, local production and service.
